01

Site Name

AcquireGH

AcquireGH combines "Acquire" — the act of purchasing or taking ownership of a business — with "GH," the universally recognized country code for Ghana. The name is direct, professional, and immediately signals both the action (acquisition) and the geography (Ghana). It positions the platform as the primary destination for startup M&A activity in Ghana while remaining short enough to be memorable and domain-friendly.

Suggested domain: acquiregh.com

02

Site Purpose

AcquireGH is a curated marketplace where Ghanaian startup founders can list their ventures for acquisition and where buyers — investors, entrepreneurs, and corporates — can discover, evaluate, and express interest in acquiring early-stage Ghanaian startups.

What the site provides

  • A browsable listings board of startups available for sale, each with key deal metrics (asking price, monthly revenue, industry, and traction stage)
  • Detailed listing profiles accessible via modal dialogs, showing extended deal information and founder notes
  • A buyer's guide page covering how acquisition deals are structured in the Ghanaian market, common deal types (full acquisition, acqui-hire, asset purchase), and what due diligence looks like at the early stage
  • A "List Your Startup" submission form for founders who are open to acquisition conversations
  • A personal watchlist feature (via local storage) allowing buyers to save and track listings they are interested in
03

Scenarios

The following questions represent real concerns from the two primary audiences of AcquireGH — buyers and sellers.

Buyer — Angel Investor

"I want to acquire a fintech startup in Ghana with some existing monthly revenue. What listings are available and what are they asking for?"

Seller — Early-Stage Founder

"I built a logistics app but I'm pivoting. How do I list my startup on AcquireGH so interested buyers can reach me?"

Buyer — Corporate Development

"What does an early-stage acquisition deal typically look like in Ghana? Is it different from international markets?"

Buyer — First-Time Acquirer

"I found a startup I'm interested in — how do I save it and come back to it later without losing track?"

04

Color Scheme

Four colors form the complete palette. Each color has a defined functional role across the interface.

Action Blue

#0061FE

Primary CTA buttons, links, active nav states, listing tags, section accent borders, and interactive highlights.

Warm Cream

#F7F5F2

Page background, card backgrounds on dark sections, subtle input field fills, and overall warm surface tone.

Deep Charcoal

#1E1919

Site header, footer, section header bars, primary headings, body text, and the hero/nav background.

Pure White

#FFFFFF

Card surfaces, modal backgrounds, form inputs, and any panel that needs clean separation from the cream background.

05

Typography

Two typefaces are used — one for display and one for body — creating a clear visual hierarchy across the interface.

Display Font — Bricolage Grotesque (Google Fonts)

Acquire Your Next Startup.

Used for: Site name / logo, page headings (h1, h2), section header labels, listing card titles, CTA headlines, and any large typographic feature elements. Weight: 700–800.

Body Font — Inter (Google Fonts)

Browse vetted early-stage Ghanaian startups available for acquisition. Connect directly with founders — no brokers, no commission.

Used for: All body copy, listing descriptions, navigation links, form labels and inputs, metadata, tags, and supporting UI text. Weight: 300–500.
